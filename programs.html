Our programs

From awareness to recovery, one continuous pathway.

Every part of our program exists to answer one question: what does a young person in Ethiopia actually need in order to get real, confidential, affordable mental health care?

In detail

Three pillars, working together

01

Community Outreach & Awareness

Our outreach team delivers structured sessions in secondary schools, colleges, and universities on mental health literacy, early symptom recognition, healthy coping strategies, and how to access help — supported by guest clinicians who answer questions on-site. Using validated tools such as the PHQ-9, we screen students with informed consent, identifying those who may benefit from further support.

FormatIn-person workshops at partner schools and universities, plus a peer-led Mental Health Club established at each institution.
ScreeningValidated tools (PHQ-9, GAD-7) administered with informed consent, in a semi-private setting.
02

Telemedicine-Enabled Clinical Care

Students who need professional support are matched with a licensed psychiatrist through our secure, in-house telemedicine platform. Registration and care are anonymous — no real name, photo, or institution identifier is required. Sessions are conducted by encrypted voice or video, whichever the patient prefers, and in-person sessions are available at our office for those who want them.

AnonymityPatients are identified only by an anonymized Case ID, visible solely to their treating clinician.
Session modelRegular check-ins tailored to patient need, with the option of in-person Saturday sessions.
03

Treatment Access Subsidy

Many patients require prescribed medication or referral to further care — an insurmountable cost for underprivileged youth. Our Treatment Access Subsidy covers these costs for eligible patients once referred and prescribed by a licensed external medical practitioner. Safe Minds Ethiopia does not diagnose or prescribe; we remove the financial barrier to care that has already been clinically authorized.

EligibilityMeans-tested, and activated only upon external referral and prescription.
PurposeEnsures "I cannot afford it" is never the reason a young person goes without treatment.

Clinical governance

Care delivered within a clearly defined scope.

Safe Minds Ethiopia operates strictly within a psychosocial counselling scope. We do not diagnose or manage severe psychiatric conditions, and we do not handle acute emergencies — all such cases are referred immediately to licensed medical or emergency services through an established referral pathway.

Clinical Supervisor A senior licensed psychiatrist provides oversight and regular case review for all clinical staff.
Safeguarding Lead Responsible for protecting every beneficiary, with particular attention to users under eighteen, who require verified parental consent.
Data protection All data is end-to-end encrypted and stored in compliance with data governance standards.
